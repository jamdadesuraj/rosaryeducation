Rosary School: Serve to Lead

A small educational institution started in 1958 named 'Rosary' in Pune, the bastion of education, has now grown into a massive banyan tree. With the motto 'Serve to Lead' etched in its very strong roots. Rosary's epic journey continues unabated to create a future generation with a dedicated spirit. The banyan tree of the organization which began with only 150 children has now blossomed and currently, the six campuses — four schools and two junior colleges! — across the city serves as a compass for thousands of students while shaping their future. Along with excellence in the field of academics, the institution is wholly committed to creating young leaders with their accent on nurturing the students mentally, physically as well as intellectually. The management of the Rosary Education Society are proud of the progress of the organization which continues to march into a bright, modern future armed with a traditional value education. It is said that from a tiny seed emerges the great banyan tree. Never has an analogy been more apt than in the case of founders AG Aranha and Winifred Aranha, who took a small step which led to a mammoth legacy that has been unparalleled in the history of education in Pune City. Without seeking any kind of financial aid from the government or from benefactors, the Aranhas with their educational bent of mind started the school in 1958 from a small building on Sachapir Road in Camp, ​ ​ Pune. Historians will record that back then there were only 150 students. However, due to quality education, insistence on excellence and teachers who believed in the holistic development of the students, the school has become a benchmark for education in the city of Pune. The Rosary School is mighty proud of the fact that it has managed to maintain the tradition of 100% results in Class X since its inception. When the quiet, little Pensioner's Paradise — Pune — was spreading its wing and metamorphosing into the Oxford of the East, parents were desperately looking for quality schools for their progeny. The children from Hadapsar, Kondhwa, Chandan Nagar all the way to Warje Salunkhe Vihar made their way to Rosary School. In order to provide them with an easy access to school in their respective jurisdiction, Rosaryi School decided to provide education in different parts of the city. Circa 2003 at Salunkhe Vihar and Viman Nagar and in 2005, at Warje the foundation of the school was laid. As mentioned earlier, at present, under the aegis of the Rosary Education Society rest nine schools and two junior colleges.. The key to the success of Rosary School is that the teaching and learning methods have marched hand in hand with times and change. Case in point during the pandemic, the Rosary Group of Institutes earned itself another laurel to feather its already crowded hat — they earned reputation of providing an excellent online education! No surprise then that the students are imparted education by adopting all kinds of technical services like fully equipped computer rooms, audio visual facilities, work sheets based on textbooks etc. A special feature of the Rosary school is its close-knit ties between the school principal, teachers, students, parents and staff. So Rosary has become a family. If the Rosary School is the heart then the alumni are its heartbeats! The management continues to nurture this relationship. It has created a fountain of enthusiasm with its tradition of student-centric education using innovative ideas and technology. The Rosary Education Society doesn't believe in the adage of all work and no play. Perhaps that's one of the many reasons why myriad extra-curricular activities and competitions are conducted to hone talent round the clock to help their young wards spread their wings and fly upwards and onwards — after all, sky is the limit! students.
