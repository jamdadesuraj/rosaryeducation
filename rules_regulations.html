RULES & REGULATIONS

  • Home About Us RULES & REGULATIONS

Best School in pune

  • Only recommended school uniform of the correct pattern and colors MUST BE WORN.
  • Orders for school uniform is must be placed in the first week of March.
Leave
  • To apply for leave of absence, the leave application in the Calendar, duly completed by the guardian, one line at a time, should be presented to the Principal as soon as the need for leave is felt.
  • If the Calendar is with the teacher, a leave letter should be submitted.
  • The Principal has the right to deny or cancel leave.
  • No leave will be granted for weddings, festivals etc. or because the parents have to go out of station.
  • Absence without leave is a grave offence deserving severe punishment such as expulsion.
  • A medical certificate if asked for by the principal must be produced from the schools certified doctor only.
Discipline:
  • Strict regularity, implicit obedience, politeness and courtesy in speech and conduct, smartness and cleanliness of dress and person are rigidly insisted upon.
  • Objectionable conduct by a child or parent whether in or out of the school is punishable with expulsion.
  • No book, periodical, calculator, mobile phone or any other article, not approved by the Principal may be brought to school.
  • Any damage done in class or about the premises has to be made good. The principal is the sole judge regarding the amount to be paid.
  • Pupils should not leave their belongings in school and are themselves responsible for their safe custody.
  • Pupils suffering from unpleasant or infectiousdiseases are not permitted to attend school.
The Calendar::
  • The Calendar must be brought to school daily and should be maintained carefully.
  • The pupil should write down daily assignments.
  • Guardians must countersign it in the space provided.
  • The margin is reserved for the remarks of the principal and of the teachers only.
  • If guardians wish to state anything, a separate letter is to be written.
  • Whenever a pupil or guardian presents himself before the Principal, the Calendar must be with him/her.
Guardians:
  • Guardians should co-operate with the school by enabling their wards to observe the rules and by insisting upon punctuality and regularity in attending school and in studying at home.
  • Communication, if any, should be addressed to the principal, not to the teachers. While writing to the principal, the pupil's standard, division and roll number should invariably be mentioned.
  • Guardians may not see their wards in school or meet the teachers without the principal’s consent.
  • In the matters of interpretation of rules, the principal’s decision is final. Guardians are reminded that, according to the school code, they cannot dictate to the school authorities, but the school authorities have the right to prescribe the conditions for admitting children and for retaining them in school.
FEES:
  • The school management reserves the right to revive the fees every academic year and the school is the final authority for the charges of various products and services.
  • All rules and regulations that would be prescribed from time to time have to be followed strictly by all students and parents, failing which, the school management has complete authority to expel the student from the school.
  • The parents have to reimburse the school, the cost of educational material, co-curricular, extra-curricular activities, school picnics, functions etc.
  • The fees are an annual fee payable by postdated cheque before 20st March every year.
GUIDELINES FOR STUDENTS
  • Newly admitted children are on probation.
  • Those who do not give satisfaction as regards:
    • Regularity
    • Cleanliness
    • Conduct
    • Studies
    • Hygienic toilet habits, or
    • Parental Co-operation will be asked to leave.
  • School uniform to be ordered in the month of March.
  • No variation in cloth, color or pattern is permitted.
  • Boys should wear shoes with laces, and girls with buckles.
  • If child falls sick the calendar should be immediately sent to the Principal for getting the leave sanctioned. Every student must be present on the last day before and the reopening day after a vacation, otherwise their name will be removed.
  • The uniform should be clean and should be ironed daily. Socks should be washed daily. Shoes should be polished daily. Finger nails should be cut every week. Boy's hair to be cut short. Girl's hair to be clean and neat.
    • We at Rosary aim at the all-round development of our students. Co-operation of the parents to develop the mental, physical, moral and leadership qualities is essential. lt is mandatory that a student participate in all activities planned by the school.
  • Individual and group activities are not only encouraged, but insisted upon.
  • All students MUST participate in all activities, whether individual or group.
  • Educational excursions are regularly organized twice a year and are compulsory.
  • All students are trained and guided to appear for various tests and evaluation examinations conducted by both Government and Non-Government Organizations.
  • Appearance for such exams is not only encouraged but insisted upon.
  • If the child suffers from any contagious diseases, it is the duty of the parent to inform the school immediately.
  • If the child suffers from illnesses like asthma or fits, the school should be kept informed and necessary medication should be kept with the child. The name and the phone number of the doctor treating the child should be informed to the school.
